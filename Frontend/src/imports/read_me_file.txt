Indian farmers lose significant income due to lack of real-time crop pricing data, forcing reliance on exploitative middlemen who capitalize on information asymmetry. Existing systems are outdated, inaccessible, and language-exclusive, leaving rural farmers disconnected from fair market opportunities and government schemes.
Proposed Solution:
KrishiConnect is a dual-channel agritech platform combining a lightweight multilingual web app with a missed-call-based SMS service — requiring zero internet or smartphone access. Farmers register crop details and harvest timelines at local Hobli divisions. A simple missed call returns real-time crop prices, verified buyer contacts, and government scheme updates in their regional language. This eliminates middlemen, ensures price transparency, and feeds grassroots agricultural data into a centralized backend for improved government planning and procurement.
